Introduction

A basic introduction, May 25, 2018

Price discrimination is the practice of selling the same product at different prices to different groups of people. Although it has existed for many years in the airline and hospitality industries, the increasing availability of consumer information has led to online retailers such as Amazon to use price discrimination.

Comparison Shopping Engines

Helpful Resources, May 26, 2018

Comparison shopping engines (CSE) are channels for collecting product information, including pricing, from participating retailers and then display that collective information on a single results page in response to a shopper's search query. In this way, shoppers can compare prices, shipping options, and service from multiple retailers on a single page and choose the merchant that offers the best overall value. For example, if a customer was looking to buy a product and wanted to see what their options are, or maybe find the best price, they can go to Google Shopping (one of the most popular CSE's around) and initiate a search. They would be able to see the same item and its different selling prices from distributors.
